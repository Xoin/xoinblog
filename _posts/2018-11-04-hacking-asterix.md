One of the few Gameboy games I had as a kid was [Asterix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asterix_(1993_video_game)), a game that does a lot of intresting stuff, but intresting rom hacking information is nowhere to be found for it. So for probably a few years now I have been tinkering about seeing how things work.
<!--more-->

### Memory

Adress | Domain | type | Description
------ | ------ | --- |-----------
 `0x23`  |  `HRAM`  | `s16_le` | player x
 `0x25`  |  `HRAM`  | `s8` | player y
 `0x48`  |  `HRAM`  | `s8` | player speed
 `0x26`  |  `HRAM`  | `s8` | player drop
 `0x18`  |  `HRAM`  | `s16_le` | offset
 `0x47`  |  `HRAM`  | `s8` | player run
 `0x29`  |  `HRAM`  | `s8` | player hurt
 `0x30`  |  `HRAM`  | `s8` | player timer

### Enemies

`0x03A0` if the place in `WRAM` where enemies are stored. Every enemy is exactly `0xD` long. Space is reserved for 20 of them, when one is destroyed it keeps all data in place until it gets overwritten when new object or enemy is triggerd.
